Semantic Markup

Semantic markup is a way of writing and structuring your HTML (Hypertext Markup Language) so that it reinforces the semantics, or meaning, of the content rather than its appearance. Elements used will tell actually what they refer to on the front side

Instead of <div>, more specific elements are used in the code.


For example:

  1. < section >
    2. Represents a generic standalone section of a document, which doesn't have a more specific semantic element to represent it.
  2. < article >
    3. Represents a self-contained composition in a document, page, application, or site, which is intended to be independently distributable or reusable
  3. < main >
    4. Represens the dominant content of the body of a document.
  4. < nav >
    5. Represents a section of a page whose purpose is to provide navigation links, either within the current document or to other documents.
  5. < headerfooter >
  6. < aside >
  7. < summary >
  8. < details >
